Mining Forum Americas — Agenda Structure Guide

Internal Reference for Schedule Building & Review

This guide documents the principles behind the MFA presentation schedule. Use it when building the agenda, reviewing alignment, or onboarding new team members to the scheduling process.

1. Stage Hierarchy

Stages are ordered by market cap preference. The largest companies present on Stage 1; the smallest on Stage 4.

StageProfileTypical Market Cap
1Stage 1 Mega-cap producers, major royalties $5B+
2Stage 2 Mid-cap producers, larger developers, royalty cluster $1B – $6B
3Stage 3 Smaller producers, developers, base metals & specialty $250M – $2B
4Stage 4 Micro-cap explorers, early-stage developers, service providers <$500M

2. Session Priority

Not all time slots are equal. The most prominent companies should be assigned to the highest-priority sessions first.

  1. Tuesday morning, Stage 1 — The prime session
  2. Monday afternoon (all stages, especially Stage 1)
  3. Monday morning (all stages)
  4. Tuesday afternoon (all stages)
  5. Tuesday morning, Stages 2–4
  6. Wednesday (all stages — smallest companies / explorers)

3. Market Cap Cadence

Within each half-day, market caps should follow an ascending-then-descending arc. This creates natural "peaks" around mid-morning and mid-afternoon.

Early Morning → ~10 AM
↗
Market cap ascending
Smallest → Largest
~10 AM → Lunch
↘
Market cap descending
Largest → Smallest
Post-Lunch → ~3 PM
↗
Market cap ascending
Smallest → Largest
~3 PM → Close
↘
Market cap descending
Largest → Smallest
Note: This is a general pattern, not an absolute rule. Minor deviations are expected. Large jumps (e.g., a $30B company among $4B peers) should be flagged unless confirmed as a deliberate placement.

4. Thematic Alignment

When grouping companies within a session block, align by these factors in priority order:

1 Status 2 Mineral 3 Market Cap 4 Geography

Status Grouping

Producers with producers, developers with developers, explorers with explorers, royalties with royalties. Status transitions within a session (e.g., producers in the morning, developers in the afternoon) are fine.

Mineral Clustering

Companies should present alongside others with the same primary mineral. Where that isn't possible, the following clusters are acceptable:

Precious Metals
Gold, Silver, PGMs — can intermix freely
Base / Energy Metals
Copper, Lithium, Uranium, Zinc, Nickel — can be grouped together as a contrast to the precious metals majority
Avoid: Placing a lithium or antimony company in an otherwise all-gold block, or a titanium company opening a copper session. These mineral orphans stand out and should be moved to a thematically compatible block.

Geography

Where status, mineral, and market cap are similar, prefer geographic clustering: Australian gold producers together, West African gold together, Latin American copper together, etc. This is the lowest-priority factor — a nice-to-have, not a requirement.

5. Session Structure

Basics

  • Sessions are defined by moderator assignment. Each moderator runs a contiguous block of 4–6 companies (up to 12 for all-day single-moderator stages).
  • Each presentation slot is 20 minutes. Session breaks appear as header rows with a moderator name but no company.
  • Within-session reordering is easy — just shuffle the rows within a moderator's block.
  • Cross-session swaps require moderator reassignment and are more disruptive.

Moderator Assignment Rules

  1. No consecutive sessions. A moderator should not run back-to-back sessions without a break. If a moderator has two sessions on the same day, separate them with at least one session gap (e.g., early AM and post-lunch, not late AM and early PM).
  2. Spread across days. When a moderator is assigned multiple sessions, distribute them across different days where possible rather than stacking them on a single day.
  3. Best-fit coverage alignment. Match moderators to sessions based on their reported coverage expertise — mineral focus, geographic specialization, or company-stage familiarity (e.g., a moderator who covers Australian gold should moderate the Australian gold producer block, not the Latin American copper block).
Exception: A single moderator (often the event lead) may run all-day blocks on lower-priority stages (Stage 3, Stage 4, Wednesday). This is acceptable when the session count exceeds available specialist moderators.

6. Common Red Flags

When reviewing a draft schedule, watch for these patterns:

⚠
MC Outlier
A company >3× or <0.33× the session median market cap
⚠
Mineral Orphan
A single base metal company in an all-gold block (or vice versa)
⚠
Cadence Break
MC jumping sharply upward in what should be a descending portion
⚠
Stage Misfit
A $1B company on Stage 1 (too small) or $3B+ on Stage 3 (too big)
⚠
Missing Data
$0 or blank market cap — needs to be populated before scheduling
⚠
Status Mismatch
An explorer slotted between mega-cap producers in a premium session
